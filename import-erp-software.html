What "import ERP" really means

Most ERP systems treat importing as an edge-case: a few fields for a shipment, then manual allocation in finance. Import businesses need the opposite. Importing is the core operation. Costs are multi-part, timing is uncertain, and decisions must be made before final costs are known.

An import ERP must connect purchase orders, shipments, landed costs, inventory receipts, and margins into one chain. Otherwise pricing and profitability decisions happen using partial truth.

FlowIQ is designed around that chain, so cost truth flows into inventory and reporting automatically. If your next bottleneck is stock visibility after landed cost is posted, see our inventory software for importers page.

What you get with FlowIQ

  • Shipment-level cost capture: supplier value, freight, duty, clearance, insurance, FX variance.
  • Allocation logic across SKUs (value, weight, CBM, or policy-based rules).
  • Receipt into inventory with landed cost applied so valuation and margins align.
  • Forecast-driven reordering to reduce stockouts and excess stock.
  • Cleaner accounting outcomes via consistent operational records.
